Sound Logo for my Chapman Stick

My sound logo is derived from, and for, my chapman stick, which is a polyphonic, 8-12 stringed instrument in the electric guitar family that I've been playing for almost 2 years. This is part of a slow process of introducing it into my compositional work.

The sound source for the logo is a recording of me playing a chord, which is then reversed (ala Roundabout). The stick has a big range (it's effectively a bass and a guitar), so I tried making the chord pretty big and spread out. This audio file is then played back a bunch of times in quick succession on top of each other. Each playback has a heavily Q'd ResonZ filter at a random freq so that you only get a small slice of the recording. This turns the reversal sound into more of a textural effect.

The progression from this piece comes from me changing the variables surrounding the file playback. As the program progresses, the rhythm gets more regular, the frequency range of the filters increases from high to low, the Q is decreased, and the panning field expands. It's closed out with the full audio file being played. The whole thing is basically a big crescendo.
